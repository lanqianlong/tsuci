This is a full scholarship program established by Ministry of Education of China to support Chinese universities to recruit outstanding international students for graduate studies in China.

Program Duration and Language of Instruction

I. 2–3 years for master’s programs; 3–4 years for doctoral programs
II. Applicants for programs in Chinese medium without HSK-4 should study 1 year Chinese at our school (also supported by the scholarship). Programs in English do not require HSK. For PhD candidates, HSK is not compulsory if your application proposal is approved by BJTU supervisors. The deadline for the TSU application is March 31, 2014.

Scholarship Coverage

I. Waiver of tuition fee and accommodation fee on campus etc.
II. Monthly living allowance 1,700 CNY for master; 2,000 CNY for doctor
III. One-off settlement subsidy after registration 1,500 CNY/Person
IV. Comprehensive Medical Insurance

Eligibility

I. Foreign citizen, in good health
II. Not be a registered student in Chinese universities at the time of application; or be a graduate from Chinese universities for more than one year;
III. Be a bachelor’s degree holder under the age of 35 when applying for master’s programs
IV. Be a master’s degree holder under the age of 40 when applying for doctoral programs

3. Academic transcripts: Transcripts in languages other than Chinese or English must be attached with notarized Chinese or English translations.
4. A Study Plan or Research Proposal in Chinese or English with a minimum of 800 words
5. Two letters of recommendation from professors or associate professors
6. Foreigner Physical Examination Form (photocopy)

7. Photocopy of passport

Qingdao University 2014 Confucius Institute Scholarship Application Information

2014 Confucius Institute Scholarship Application Information

I. Scholarship Category and Application Requirements

Being authorized as one of hosting universities of Confucius Institute Scholarship (CIS), the 2014 QU CIS category is as follows:

·15 for Students of Master's Degree in Teaching Chinese to Speakers of Other Languages

·8 for One-Academic-Year Students

·8 for One-Semester Students

Applicants shall be non-Chinese citizens aged between 18 and 35 (Employed Chinese language teachers shall be aged below 45).

1. Scholarship for Students of Master's Degree in Teaching Chinese to Speakers of Other Languages（15）

The scholarship is provided to students of Confucius Institutes (Classrooms), outstanding performers on HSK exams, winners of the "Chinese Bridge” Chinese Proficiency Competitions for Foreign College Students and for Foreign High School Students, Chinese language teachers from countries across the world, and outstanding graduates of Chinese language across the world for taking the MTCSOL degree. The scholarship winner shall begin the study in autumn 2014, and the grant will cover two academic years. After finishing the study, students will obtain the graduate diploma and be conferred with the Master’s degree certificate in MTCSOL.

Applicants are required to have bachelor degrees or its equivalent with a score of at least 180 on the new HSK (level 5), HSKK Intermediate scores of at least 50 points, and make a written commitment stating that he/she will be engaged in Chinese language teaching for at least 5 years after graduation. Those with specific plans for teaching at an institution should provide proof from their institution that the institution plans to employ them. Students who can provide such proof will be given admission preference. After completing the MTCSOL program, Hanban will provide support and assistance to graduates in seeking employment teaching Chinese.

2. Scholarship for One-Academic Year Students（8）

The scholarship is provided to outstanding students of Confucius Institutes (Classrooms), outstanding performers on HSK, winners of the “Chinese Bridge” Chinese Proficiency Competitions for Foreign College Students and for Foreign High School Students, Chinese language teachers worldwide, and outstanding graduates of Chinese language across the world, or those who intend to become Chinese teachers. The scholarship winner may choose to study majors including Chinese Language and Literature, Chinese History and Chinese Philosophy. The time of entrance is autumn 2014 and the grant term is one academic year.

Applicants should achieve a score of at least 180 on the new HSK (level 3) and a score of at least 60 on the HSKK Elementary.

3. Scholarship for One-Semester Students（8）

The scholarship is provided to outstanding students of Confucius Institutes (Classrooms), outstanding performers on HSK, winners of the “Chinese Bridge” Chinese Proficiency Competitions for Foreign College Students and for Foreign High School Students, Chinese language teachers worldwide, and outstanding students of Chinese language across the world. The scholarship winner may choose to study majors including Chinese Language and Literature, Chinese History and Chinese Philosophy. The time of entrance is autumn 2014 or Spring 2015 and the grant term is one semester.

Applicants should achieve a score of at least 120 on the new HSK (level 2) and achieve a score of at least 40 on the HSKK Elementary.

II. Scholarship Coverage and Criteria

Scholarship winners are exempt from registration fees, tuition, fees，basic learning materials, accommodation fees on campus, and are provided with monthly allowance，one-off resettlement subsidy, outpatient medical service and comprehensive insurance for foreign students studying in China. Allowance is ￥1400.00/month for language students, ￥1700.00 for master program students. One-off resettlement fee is ￥1500.00 for over one year study and ￥1000.00 for one semester. The one-off resettlement subsidy will not be provided to those who have studied in China for more than half a year before being enrolled.

III. Application Procedures

1. Applicants should log on to the Confucius Institute Scholarship website at http://cis.chinese.cn, set up an individual account, read information and introduction of host institutes and majors, complete and submit the Confucius Institute Scholarship Application Form online, print out the Form and submit it to their recommending institution along with other application materials (see Article 4).

2. Recommendation agencies: Confucius Institutes (independently set-up Confucius Classrooms), overseas Chinese test centers, educational and cultural offices (sections) of Chinese embassies (consulates), host institutes and institutions of higher education in those countries with no Confucius Institutes are entrusted by Hanban as the recommending institutions. The recommending institutions shall exam the qualification and application materials of applicants, and recommend outstanding applicants, and present a list of recommended students to Hanban before May 10th.

3. Qingdao University is responsible for reviewing the qualification of applicants and submitting the proposed enrollment name list of qualified candidates to Hanban by June 26th.

4. Hanban will organize an expert panel to make the final selection and publish the name list of successful applicants on the Scholarship website by June 16th, which will be informed to applicants through recommending institutions.

5. Qingdao University will post “Admission Notice”, “Enrollment Instructions”, “Visa Application Form for Foreigners to Study in China" (JW202 Form) and other related materials to successful applicants through their recommending institutions by July 1st 2012.

IV. Application Documents

Applicants must register online on the scholarship website. Fill out the Confucius Institute Scholarship Application Form and attach all additional digital certification materials.

1. Confucius Institute Scholarship Application Form

2. Photocopy of Passport Photo Page

3. Application Statement: stating applicant's Chinese language learning background, study plan and aim in China It shall be written in Chinese and no less than 800 words.

4. HSK and HSKK Result Report

5.Resume (Please download the Resume template from the Qingdao University International Students website: http://www.international.qdu.edu.cn/Info/?id=42)

6. Applicants shall submit notarized highest education diplomas attained or proof of study (certificate of upcoming graduation or certificate that you are currently studying).

7. Reference Letter and Letter of Commitment. Students attending Chinese language programs of universities in countries with no Confucius Institutes established shall attach a reference letter signed and issued by the university President. The Master’s Degree Scholarship applicants are required to submit reference letters provided by two professors or associate professors (in Chinese or English). A written commitment stating that the applicant will be engaged in Chinese language teaching for at least 5 years after graduation (written in Chinese and sign with signature).

8. Applications under the age of 18 shall submit relevant legal documents of entrusted legal guardians in China.

9. Winners of the finals of the “Chinese Bridge” Chinese Proficiency Competitions for Foreign College Students and for Foreign High School Students in China shall submit the duplicate of scholarship certificate. Winners of preliminary rounds of the “Chinese Bridge” Proficiency Competitions in their countries shall provide award proof and reference letter provided by the organizers.

10. Chinese language teachers shall submit the proof of their employment and a letter of recommendation from the institute they work for.

V. Entrance and Annual Appraisal

1. The scholarship winners shall register at the host institutes before the deadline set by the institutes. Scholarships will not be kept for those who do not register on time without reasonable cause.

2. Scholarships will be canceled for those who cannot pass the health examination.

3. Students for a Master’s degree in Teaching Chinese to Speakers of Other Languages (MTCSOL) shall be subject to an annual appraisal organized by the host institutes in the second semester after being enrolled according to the “Annual Appraisal Procedures for Confucius Institute Scholarship”. Only the eligible students are entitled to continue to receive the Scholarships in the following year.

Application for Sino-American Cultural Exchange Scholarship 2014，Xiamen University

Dear Director,

Greetings from the Admissions Office of Xiamen University in China!

As application for Sino-American Cultural Exchange Scholarship has opened, we would like to take this opportunity to give you a brief introduction to this scholarship.

Sino-American Cultural Exchange Scholarship is sponsored by the Ministry of Education of China, in response to President Barack Obama's proposal of sending 100,000 American students to study in China. The programme plans to finance a total of 10,000 American students, either for degree studies or just for non-degree credits.

Xiamen University is among the first universities affiliated to Ministry of Education to accept Sino-American Cultural Exchange Scholarship students. The scholarship provides full funds for full-time American master's or doctoral candidates admitted by XMU. Holders of this scholarship are exempt from tuition and are given subsidies for accommodation, food, medical and insurance costs.

The application deadline for Sino-American Cultural Exchange Scholarship is April 15, 2014. More information can be found at http://admissions.xmu.edu.cn/en/show/92.html.

A glance at XMU

Xiamen University (XMU), located in Xiamen, a coastal city in southeast China, boasts one of the most beautiful campuses in the world. XMU is recognized as a first-class national university with an international reputation as a centre of scholarly excellence, and ranks among the top 15 of the over 2,000 universities and colleges in China. Currently XMU hosts over 3,000 international students from more than 100 countries.

XMU is becoming increasingly internationalized and is actively engaged in multilateral and global cooperation. It has established partnerships with over 300 higher-learning institutions around the world, 36 of which are in America. XMU operates 15 Confucius Institutes around the world, which play an important role in promoting cultural exchanges between China and the rest of the world. Students during their studies at XMU have an opportunity to participate in academic exchange programmes or pursue further studies at world-renowned universities.

Currently, XMU offers 15 master's programmes and over 60 doctoral programmes in English for international students. Meanwhile, there are a number of Chinese-medium postgraduate programmes on offer. With its highly professional faculty, well-established disciplines, first-class facilities and garden-like campuses, XMU offers a great opportunity for American students.
