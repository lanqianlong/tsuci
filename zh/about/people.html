Dr. Roger Hart Director

BIO

Roger Hart is director of the Texas Southern University Confucius Institute and an associate professor in Chinese history. He is author of The Chinese Roots of Linear Algebra (Johns Hopkins University Press, 2010) and Imagined Civilizations: China, the West, and Their First Encounter (Johns Hopkins University Press, 2013). He has received fellowships from ACLS, NEH, and Mellon. Previous appointments include Seoul National University, University of Texas at Austin, University of Chicago, Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, Stanford, UC Berkeley, and Harvard. He earned his PhD from UCLA in history, and his MS from Stanford and BS from MIT in mathematics.

For more information see http://rhart.org

Dr. Xiao YI Chinese Director

BIO

Dr. Xiao YI 易晓 is the Chinese Director the Texas Southern University Confucius Institute. She is professor and chair of the Department of Design in the School of Architecture and Design at Beijing Jiaotong University. She earned her Ph.D. from Tsinghua University, her M.A. from Hubei Institute of Fine Arts, and her B.E. from Hunan University. Her research interests include design and design history. She was a visiting professor at Brunel University (UK) and University of Twente (The Netherlands). Yu Chang Visiting Scholar

BIO

Dr. Danille K. Taylor Dean-College of Liberal and Behavioral Sciences

BIO

Dr. Taylor attended Princeton University where she was an English major with a concentration in Afro American Studies. Taylor went to graduate school at Boston University where she earned a M.A. in African American Studies. Continuing her graduate studies at Brown University in American Civilization she earned a M.A. and Ph.D. Her areas of specialization are African American and African diaspora cultures, African American literature, ethnic literature and womanist studies. Dr. Taylor has taught at The School of the Art Institute, University of Illinois Chicago, Northwestern University and was the chair of Minority Studies at Indiana University Northwest. In 2010 Dr. Taylor became the dean of the College of Liberal Arts and Behavioral Sciences at Texas Southern University in Houston, Texas. The revitalization of the largest college at the university is her goal. She has already begun to engage in the civic life of Houston having been appointed by the mayor to the Board of Directors of Miller Outdoor Theatre.

For more information see TSU.edu

Dr. Ronald A. Johnson Dean-The Jesse H. Jones School of Business

BIO

Dr. Ronald A. Johnson is the dean of the Jesse H. Jones School of Business, at Texas Southern University. Dr. Johnson holds a Ph.D. and MA in Economics from Stanford University, and an MBA in Finance and a BA in Economics from Adelphi University in New York. Dr. Johnson is the former president and chief investment officer at Smith Graham & Co. in Houston, Texas and has served as director of global fixed income research and senior portfolio manager for Templeton Worldwide Inc. in Fort Lauderdale, Florida and chief strategist and head of research for Americas Trust Bank in Miami, Florida. Dr. Johnson has held positions with the Federal Reserve Bank of New York, and with the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System and the International Monetary Fund, both in Washington, D.C.

Dr. James W. Ward Dean-College of Communication

BIO

Dr. James W. Ward, 55, is the Dean of Texas Southern University’s Tavis Smiley School of Communication. A tenured professor for more than 25 years, Dr. Ward taught at Texas Tech University, Highland Park College, and the University of Texas at Austin before returning to Texas Southern University, his alma mater. After earning his B.A. degree, Ward studied organization communication and small group behavior at Texas Tech University. There, he earned an M.A. degree, which was quickly followed by his earning a Ph.D. in sociolinguistics, rhetoric, and public address from Wayne State University in Detroit, Michigan. Dr. Ward studies the comparative differences of linguistic behaviors of those in various cultures.

For more information see TSU.edu

Dannye R. Holley Dean-Thurgood Marshall School of Law

BIO

Dean and Professor Dannye Holley received his B.A. and J.D. from the State University of New York, Buffalo; and his LL.M. from the University of California, Berkeley. This is his fortieth year of law school teaching, and he currently teaches Criminal Law, Advanced Criminal Law and Procedure, and Evidence. He has served as a Faculty member for each of the first three Texas Center for the Judiciary’s Evidence Summits for sitting Texas Judges. His recent publications have focused on culpability standards and evaluations by the federal and states supreme courts, the constitutionality of the criminalization of suspicion, and the evidence rule standards regulating the admission of convictions to impeach.
